# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or VP of Engineering at a seed-to-Series A startup (10-50 employees, 3-15 engineers). They pay for 3+ AI tools across the team. No dedicated finance ops or procurement person. The EM approves tool spend via expense reports or a shared company card and rarely audits whether each subscription is still needed or right-sized.

**Secondary:** Solo technical founders running a team of 2-5 where they personally pay for all the tools and feel the bill every month.

## What They Search Before Needing This

They do not search "AI spend audit" because that category does not exist in their head yet. Instead, they search:

- "cursor vs copilot 2025" (comparing, considering a switch)
- "is claude pro worth it for a team"
- "chatgpt team vs enterprise difference"
- "reduce saas costs startup"
- "how much should an engineering team spend on AI tools"

These are the SEO keywords and content angles that drive inbound. The tool answers the question they are actually asking: "am I paying too much for this?"

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **Reddit:** r/ExperiencedDevs (340K members, heavily moderated), r/startups (1.1M, active weekly threads), r/SideProject
- **Hacker News:** Show HN posts with honest framing get consistent traction. The audience is exactly right.
- **X/Twitter:** Follow YC founders, indie hackers, AI tool reviewers. Quote-tweet threads about AI tool costs with the audit link.
- **Slack groups:** Ramen Club (bootstrapped founders), Lenny's Newsletter Slack, OnDeck Engineering
- **Discord:** Cursor community, Windsurf community, various "AI builders" servers
- **LinkedIn:** Engineering managers post about tooling decisions. Direct outreach works here.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)

**Week 1 (Days 1-7):**
1. Submit a Show HN post: "Show HN: I built a free AI spend audit for startups." Target: 30-50 audits from HN alone.
2. Post in r/ExperiencedDevs weekly discussion thread as a comment in the "tools" thread, not as a link post.
3. Cold DM 30 engineering managers on LinkedIn: "Noticed your team uses Cursor and Copilot — we built a free tool that checks if you are on the right plan. No login needed." Target: 5-10 complete the audit.

**Week 2 (Days 8-14):**
4. Post in 3 Slack groups with a value-first frame: "We audited 50 startups' AI spend this week. Average savings was $340/mo. Here is the free tool."
5. Reply to every tweet about AI tool pricing with a non-spammy response + link. Target: 10-15 audits from Twitter.
6. Post a comparison thread on X: "I compared every AI coding tool's pricing. Here is what most startups get wrong."

**Week 3 (Days 15-21):**
7. Submit to Product Hunt. Write a proper launch post with before/after screenshots. Target: 20-30 audits from PH.
8. Write a blog post: "We audited 100 startups' AI spend. Here is what we found." Data-driven content from anonymized audit data.

**Week 4 (Days 22-30):**
9. Follow up with every lead who completed an audit but did not convert.
10. Ask 5 users who found real savings to share their audit link on Twitter/LinkedIn.

**Total target:** 100+ audits, 25+ emails captured, 5-8 consultation requests.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that buy AI credits. The sales team can embed SpendSight into their cold outreach as a free value-add: "Before you buy credits from us, audit your current spend for free." This turns a sales pitch into a genuine gift. The audit output naturally surfaces Credex as the savings mechanism.

No competitor can replicate this because they do not have the credit supply side. The audit tool plus the ability to actually deliver savings through discounted credits is the full loop.
