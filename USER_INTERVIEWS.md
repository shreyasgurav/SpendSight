# User Interviews

Three conversations conducted over WhatsApp/call with hostel friends who currently pay for AI tools.

## Interview 1 — Keshav Mallawat, CS Student + Part-time Indie Hacker

**Context:** Keshav is in his final year CS, building side projects on weekends. He uses Cursor Pro ($20/mo) for coding, ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) for general queries, and pays for Vercel Pro ($20/mo) to deploy his projects. All out of his own pocket from internship savings.

**Key quotes:**
- "I am burning $60 a month on tools and I don't even track which one I use more. My friend just uses Windsurf free and gets 90% of what I need."
- "I would downgrade today if someone just showed me the math side by side. I am too lazy to open 4 pricing pages."
- "The worst part is I pay for ChatGPT but half my prompts I could just do on Claude free since I am not hitting limits."

**Insight:** Students/indie hackers feel every dollar because it is their own money, not a company card. They want the specific "switch to X, save Y" without having to research themselves. The free tiers of competitors (Windsurf, Claude) are genuinely good enough for many use cases.

## Interview 2 — Namit Lalwani, ML Research Intern at a Startup

**Context:** Namit is doing a 6-month ML internship. His team of 4 shares one ChatGPT Team subscription ($30/user = $120/mo company pays). He personally pays for Claude Pro ($20/mo) for research paper reading and Cursor Pro ($20/mo) for coding.

**Key quotes:**
- "My company pays for ChatGPT Team but honestly only 2 of us use it regularly. The other two just use free GPT when they need something."
- "I pay $40 out of pocket on top of what my company pays. That's like my week's food budget."
- "I don't even know if I need Cursor Pro or if I could just use Copilot which my company might pay for."

**Insight:** The overlap between personal and company tools is messy. People pay personally for tools that *feel* personal (Claude for research, Cursor for side coding) even when their company already pays for alternatives. The audit needs to catch this "double paying" scenario.

## Interview 3 — Laksh Rathi, Final Year Student + Freelance Dev

**Context:** Laksh does freelance web dev to cover hostel expenses. He has 2-3 clients at any time. Uses Cursor Pro ($20/mo), ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo), and recently started using Gemini Advanced ($20/mo) because a client asked for Gemini-specific output.

**Key quotes:**
- "I just added Gemini last month because one client wanted it. Now I am at $60/mo and I barely use Gemini after that project ended. I just forgot to cancel."
- "I have three tools doing the same thing. I justify it as 'client needs' but honestly I could do 90% of the work with just one."
- "If a tool told me 'you paid $20 for Gemini but used it 3 times this month', I would cancel immediately."

**Insight:** Freelancers accumulate tools project-by-project and forget to cancel. They need the usage-based reality check — showing not just "you could save" but "you paid for this and barely used it."

## Common Themes

1. **Personal money hurts more than company money.** All three pay out of pocket and feel every dollar. They are more motivated to audit than someone with a company card.

2. **Forgotten subscriptions are common.** Laksh added Gemini for one client and never canceled. People need a nudge to review what they signed up for.

3. **Free tiers are good enough.** Keshav's friend uses Windsurf free. Many pay for Pro plans they do not actually need.

4. **The side-by-side comparison is the value.** All three said they would switch if someone just showed them the math. They do not want to do the research themselves.
