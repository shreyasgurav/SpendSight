# User Interviews

Three conversations conducted over WhatsApp/call with people who currently pay for AI tools.

## Interview 1 — Aarav, Backend Developer at an early-stage startup (5 engineers)

**Context:** Aarav's team uses GitHub Copilot Business ($19/user) and ChatGPT Team ($30/user) for all 5 engineers. He also personally uses Cursor Pro ($20/mo) for side projects.

**Key quotes:**
- "I honestly have no idea if we need Copilot Business or if Individual would be fine. My manager just picked Business when we set it up."
- "We are paying for ChatGPT Team but I think only 2 out of 5 people actually use it regularly. The other 3 just use the free version."
- "I would definitely use a tool like this if it just told me: here is what you should cancel. Right now nobody owns this decision."

**Insight:** The decision-maker (EM) is not the daily user. The daily users know what is underutilized but have no channel to surface it. SpendSight needs to be shareable so an IC can send the results to their manager.

## Interview 2 — Priya, Freelance Full-Stack Developer

**Context:** Priya pays for Cursor Pro ($20/mo) and Claude Pro ($20/mo) out of pocket. She considered switching to Windsurf but has not tried it.

**Key quotes:**
- "I pay $40/mo on AI tools and I am not even sure if I need both. Cursor does coding, Claude does everything else. But there is overlap."
- "What would make me switch is seeing the actual price difference side by side. I do not want to go to 5 different pricing pages."
- "If you could tell me 'you can get 90% of what you need for $15/mo instead of $40/mo', I would switch today."

**Insight:** Solo developers feel the cost acutely because it comes out of their own pocket. They want a direct "do this instead" recommendation, not a dashboard. The audit's value is in the specific action, not the data.

## Interview 3 — Rohan, CTO at a seed-stage startup (3 engineers + 2 non-technical)

**Context:** Rohan's company uses Cursor Business ($40/user x 3), ChatGPT Plus ($20/user x 5), and pays for Anthropic API directly (~$200/mo).

**Key quotes:**
- "Our AI tool bill is spread across 3 different credit cards and a company card. I genuinely do not know the total."
- "I put everyone on Cursor Business because I thought we needed the admin dashboard. Turns out we never use it."
- "The API spend is the scariest part. I have no idea if $200/mo for Anthropic is normal for a team our size."

**Insight:** The biggest pain is not knowing the total. Rohan was surprised when I added up his spend ($440/mo). The "aha moment" in SpendSight should be showing the aggregate number first, then breaking it down. Also: Cursor Business for 3 people is a clear downgrade opportunity — Pro at $20/user saves $60/mo immediately.

## Common Themes

1. **Nobody owns the AI tool budget.** It is split across individuals and no one aggregates it.
2. **People stay on plans they were defaulted into.** Business/Team plans chosen at setup and never revisited.
3. **The comparison is the hard part.** Users know alternatives exist but researching pricing across 5+ vendors is tedious.
4. **Shareability matters.** The person who finds the savings is not always the person who can act on them.
